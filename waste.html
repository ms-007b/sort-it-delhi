1. RED DUSTBIN
    • Used needles and syringes (sharps)
    • Used bandages and dressings
    • Contaminated gloves
    • Surgical masks (contaminated)
    • Used cotton swabs or cotton balls (contaminated)
    • Expired medications and their packaging
    • Thermometers containing mercury
    • Blood-soaked materials (e.g., tissues, gauze)
    • Used diapers
    • Broken medical equipment (e.g., glass thermometers)
    • Contaminated gauze pads
    • Used sanitary napkins or tampons
    • Biohazardous waste from medical treatments
    • Infected animal carcasses or body parts (e.g., veterinary clinics)
    • Contaminated glass vials or ampoules
    • Chemicals (e.g., used cleaning agents or pesticides)
    • Used needles or scalpel blades
    • Pathological waste (e.g., tissue samples)
    • Expired or broken medical devices (e.g., catheters, IV tubes)
    • Infected surgical or diagnostic waste
  1. GREEN DUSTBIN
    • Fruit and vegetable peels
    • Tea bags and coffee grounds
    • Eggshells
    • Used paper napkins and tissues (if not contaminated with chemicals)
    • Food scraps (e.g., leftovers, cooked food)
    • Plant trimmings and leaves
    • Grass clippings
    • Flowers and flower petals
    • Coffee filters
    • Cardboard from food packaging (if not wax-coated)
    • Biodegradable packaging material (e.g., food containers made of cornstarch or paper)
    • Wooden toothpicks
    • Animal bedding (e.g., from pets like hamsters or rabbits)
    • Cooked rice and pasta
    • Coconut shells and husks
    • Banana peels
    • Corn cobs and husks
    • Used paper towels (if not heavily contaminated)
    • Tree branches (small, if not large or treated)
    • Natural fibers like jute or cotton (if they are compostable)
  1. BLUE DUSTBIN
    • Newspapers and magazines
    • Cardboard boxes (clean and flattened)
    • Plastic bottles and containers (clean and dry)
    • Glass bottles and jars (clean and dry)
    • Aluminum cans and tins (clean and dry)
    • Plastic bags (clean and dry)
    • Plastic wrappers and packaging (clean)
    • Old books and notebooks (without plastic covers)
    • Paper food containers (e.g., pizza boxes if not soiled)
    • Greeting cards (without metallic or plastic elements)
    • Envelopes (without plastic windows)
    • Clean cardboard packaging (e.g., cereal boxes)
    • Magazines and catalogs
    • Plastic straws (clean)
    • Paper cups (if not lined with plastic)
    • Cartons (such as milk or juice cartons, when cleaned)
    • Broken plastic toys (if made of recyclable plastic)
    • Plastic trays (clean)
    • Paper towels (if not soiled with grease or chemicals)
    • Tin foil (cleaned of food residue)
