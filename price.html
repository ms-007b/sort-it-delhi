Plastic

Plastic waste is a growing problem due to a lack of awareness, infrastructure, and technical skills for managing it. The world produces around 350 million tonnes of plastic waste each year, and about 0.5% of it ends up in the ocean.
Glass

Waste glass is another waste material that is produced in large quantities and is difficult to eliminate. It is known that most of the waste glass is collected, especially container glasses, remelted, and used to produce new glass. However, not all of the waste glass is suitable for the production of new glass.
Metal

Metal waste can be recycled by remelting, recasting, and redrawing it within a steel mill. This process is cheaper than producing new metal from ore.
Others

"Other waste" typically refers to materials that do not fit into common waste categories like recyclables, organic waste, or hazardous waste.other waste that is unusual in nature and does not meet the criteria of other types of waste. It can include large, bulky items like furniture or appliances that do not fit into typical waste streams, or materials that need specialized handling.
